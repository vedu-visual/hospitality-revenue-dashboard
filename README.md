📋 Project OverviewAtli-Q Hotels, a prominent chain in the hospitality industry, was losing market share and revenue due to strategic gaps and ineffective decision-making. This Power BI dashboard provides the Revenue Management team with data-driven insights to track performance, optimize pricing, and regain their competitive edge.Data Duration: May 2024 – July 2024📊 Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)Revenue: Total revenue generated ($2\text{bn}$).RevPAR (Revenue Per Available Room): Measures the ability of a hotel to fill its available rooms at the average rate ($7,347$).ADR (Average Daily Rate): The average rental income per paid occupied room ($12.70\text{K}$).Occupancy %: The percentage of available rooms that were stayed in ($57.87\%$).Realisation %: The percentage of successful check-outs out of the total bookings made ($70.15\%$).DSRN (Daily Sellable Room Nights): The average number of rooms available to sell per day ($2,528$).💡 Business Insights1. Revenue SegmentationLuxury vs. Business: The Luxury category is the primary revenue driver, contributing 61.61% of total revenue.Strategy: Marketing efforts should continue to prioritize luxury segments while exploring "Bleisure" (Business + Leisure) packages to boost the 38.39% business share.2. Booking Platform PerformancePlatform Efficiency: Realisation rates hover around 70% across most platforms.Direct vs. Third-Party: "Direct Online" and "Direct Offline" show consistent ADR. Shifting more bookings to direct channels can significantly reduce commission costs paid to third-party aggregators.3. Pricing Trends (ADR vs. RevPAR)The dashboard shows a flat ADR trend across weeks, yet RevPAR and Occupancy fluctuate.Insight: This suggests a fixed pricing strategy. Implementing Dynamic Pricing (raising rates during high-demand weekends) could capture more value without negatively impacting occupancy.4. Regional PerformanceMumbai is the top-performing city in terms of pure revenue ($117\text{M}$ for top properties).Bangalore and Hyderabad show high occupancy rates but lower ADR in certain properties, indicating a potential "underpricing" issue in those specific markets.🛠️ Tech Stack & SkillsTool: Power BI DesktopData Modeling: Star Schema (Fact and Dimension tables).DAX (Data Analysis Expressions): Used for creating calculated columns and measures (RevPAR, ADR, Realisation %, etc.).Data Visualization: Conditional formatting, trend lines, donut charts, and interactive slicers.UI/UX: Designed for high "scannability" using a clean, professional grey-and-white theme.📂 Dataset HierarchyThe project utilizes the following tables:dim_date: Date-wise breakdown (Week No, Month).dim_hotels: Property details (Property ID, Name, Category, City).dim_rooms: Room classifications (Elite, Premium, Standard, Presidential).fact_bookings: Individual booking transactions.fact_aggregated_bookings: Aggregated daily data for capacity and check-ins.🚀 How to UseFilter by City: Use the top slicer to drill down into specific regional performance (e.g., Mumbai vs. Delhi).Room Type Analysis: Filter by room type to see which categories (e.g., Presidential) drive the highest ADR.Trend Monitoring: Use the "Trends by Key Metrics" chart to identify specific weeks where occupancy dropped.
